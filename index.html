The Hottest States

This choropleth map indicates which of the Australian states and territories is the warmest on average in February of 2016. With February being the last month of Summer, it is interesting to note which of the states is the hottest. It it evident that the Northern Territory (33.5 degrees) is the hottest state while Tasmania (23.8 degrees) is the coldest.

The boxplots on the left reveal the distribution of daily maximum temperatures for each capital city between 2009 and 2016. Melbourne has the largest maxium temperature of 46.8 degrees, which occured during the infamous 2009 Black Saturday bushfires. Although Darwin has the largest median temperature, it also has the smallest maximum temperature in Australia and never exceeds 38 degrees.
